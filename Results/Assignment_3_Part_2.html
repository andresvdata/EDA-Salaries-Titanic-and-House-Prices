Overview

Variable Notes

pclass: A proxy for socio-economic status (SES)

  • 1st = Upper
  • 2nd = Middle
  • 3rd = Lower

age: Age is fractional if less than 1.

sibsp: The dataset defines family relations in this way:

  • Sibling = brother, sister, stepbrother, stepsister
  • Spouse = husband, wife (mistresses and fiancs were ignored)

parch: The dataset defines family relations in this way:

  • Parent = mother, father
  • Child = daughter, son, stepdaughter, stepson
    Some children travelled only with a nanny, therefore parch=0 for them.

Hypotesis

Once we did the EDA, we have enough understanding of the data to test the hypothesis

Determine if the survival rate is associated to the class of passenger

The hypothesis will be test looks like:

$H_0$: Survival rate and class of passenger are independent to each other among all subjects in the population

$H_a$: Survival rate and class of passenger are NOT independent to each other among all subjects in the population

Determine the survival rate is associated to the age

The hypothesis will be test looks like:

$H_0$: Survival rate and age are independent to each other among all subjects in the population

$H_a$: Survival rate and age are NOT independent to each other among all subjects in the population

Even though the variable Age is numerical, doing a data analysis with range of ages would be more insightful than only the ages as integers.
The ranges to change this variable into categorical with 5 levels will be like this:

  • minors : 0 to 17
  • young : 18 to 29
  • adults : 30 to 44
  • adults-2 : 45 to 60
  • elderly : greater than 60
  • No values : null
